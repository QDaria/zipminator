# Norwegian Quantum Initiative

## Overview

Announced on 27 August 2025 by Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Store in Trondheim, the Norwegian Quantum Initiative is a major national investment in quantum technology. The Research Council of Norway is responsible for designing and launching the program in 2026.

The initiative aims to mobilize large industrial players to lead an industry-rooted technology community, with embedded business cooperation in funding calls.

**Total commitment:** NOK 1.75 billion (NOK 1.1B government + NOK 750M earmarked by the Labour Party for business and research)
**Duration:** 5+ years
**Annual ongoing investment:** NOK 70M/year

## Eligibility

Exact eligibility criteria will be published when the Research Council releases the first call (expected H1/H2 2026). Based on the announced structure:

- **Industry-led consortia** are prioritized (large industrial players leading, with SME participation)
- Companies must commit to **5 years of joint development** with embedded business cooperation
- **Co-financing from participating companies** is required
- Research institutions (NTNU, UiO, SINTEF, FFI) expected as mandatory partners
- Projects must address one or more of the initiative's priority areas
- Norwegian-registered entities required as consortium leads

### Expected Priority Areas
- Quantum computing hardware and software
- **Quantum-safe cryptography and communications** (directly relevant to Zipminator)
- Quantum sensing
- Quantum networking / quantum key distribution (QKD)
- Quantum simulation for materials/pharma

## Deadline

- **First call expected:** H2 2026 (the Research Council is designing the framework throughout 2026)
- Monitor: https://www.forskningsradet.no/en/call-for-proposals/

## Amount

- Individual project grants: estimated NOK 10-50M+ (based on comparable national quantum initiatives)
- The initiative targets large-scale, multi-year consortia
- Co-financing ratio: likely 50% industry / 50% public (based on Research Council norms for industry-led projects)
- Total first-wave allocation: unknown, but likely NOK 200-500M in the initial call

## Application Requirements

Exact requirements will follow the Research Council's call text. Expected structure based on comparable programs:

2. **5-year project plan** with milestones, deliverables, and go/no-go gates

#### 3. QDaria's Contribution to the Consortium

QDaria brings the most mature PQC technology platform in Norway:

- **Zipminator Platform:** 300K+ LOC, production-grade Kyber768 engine in Rust
- **8 Product Modules:** Encrypted messaging, file vault, VPN, browser, VoIP, email, device shield, key management

**Year 5: Leadership**
- Full production across all partner organizations
- Norwegian PQC standard recommendations to NSM/Datatilsynet
