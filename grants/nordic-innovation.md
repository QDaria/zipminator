# Nordic Innovation

## Overview

Nordic Innovation is a Nordic institution under the Nordic Council of Ministers that promotes cross-border trade and innovation in the Nordic region (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, Sweden, plus Faroe Islands, Greenland, Aaland). They fund collaborative projects that strengthen the Nordic region's competitiveness.

Two current programs are relevant:

1. **Innovative Solutions for 2030:** Faster commercialization and green transition in high-impact sectors.
2. **Nordic Forward: Resilience and Competitiveness for 2050:** Long-term competitiveness through new technologies, deep-tech, and new collaboration structures. Supports ecosystems and value chain collaboration.

**Website:** https://www.nordicinnovation.org/
**Programs:** https://www.nordicinnovation.org/programs

## Eligibility

- Projects must involve partners from **at least 3 Nordic countries**
- All types of organizations: companies, research institutions, public sector, NGOs
- Project lead must be from a Nordic country
- Projects must have clear cross-Nordic relevance and impact
- Matching co-funding equal to or exceeding Nordic Innovation's contribution required
- Priority: projects that accelerate commercialization, build ecosystems, and address global/Nordic societal challenges

### Nordic PQC Ecosystem: Cross-Nordic Post-Quantum Cryptography Commercialization

#### 1. Project Title

**NordicPQC: Building the Nordic Post-Quantum Cryptography Ecosystem**

#### 2. Project Summary

NordicPQC will create a cross-Nordic ecosystem for post-quantum cryptography commercialization, connecting PQC technology providers, critical infrastructure operators, research institutions, and cybersecurity agencies across 4 Nordic countries. Led by QDaria AS (Norway) with partners in Finland, Sweden, and Denmark, the project will establish shared PQC testing infrastructure, develop joint go-to-market strategies for global export, and train Nordic cybersecurity professionals in PQC deployment.

#### 4. Cross-Nordic Added Value

The Nordic region has unique advantages for building a PQC ecosystem:
- **Finland:** NATO's newest member, strong quantum research (VTT, IQM), DIANA accelerator site
- **Sweden:** Ericsson (global telecom infrastructure), KTH (leading PQC research)
- **Denmark:** Cryptomathic (PKI leader), Alexandra Institute (applied crypto research)
- **Norway:** QDaria/Zipminator (PQC platform), Kongsberg (defense), national quantum initiative
- **Shared:** Similar regulatory frameworks, high digital maturity, NATO members (except historic exception), trusted security cooperation

No single Nordic country has the full ecosystem. Together, the Nordics can build a globally competitive PQC industry.
